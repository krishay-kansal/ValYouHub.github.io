🧠 What IRR Really Tells Us
🎯
Break-Even Rate
The exact return rate where NPV = 0. Your minimum acceptable return.
⚖️
Decision Benchmark
Compare IRR with your required return to make investment decisions.
📈
Profitability Measure
Higher IRR means better returns. It's your investment's "annual percentage yield".
IRR is the rate that makes the present value of inflows equal to outflows.
🔍 The IRR Discovery Process

IRR is found through trial and error - testing different rates until NPV = 0:

5%
Trial Rate: 5%
NPV = +₹15,000
Too Low!
10%
Trial Rate: 10%
NPV = +₹5,000
Still Too Low!
15%
Trial Rate: 15%
NPV = ₹0
Perfect! IRR = 15%
20%
Trial Rate: 20%
NPV = -₹8,000
Too High!
IRR is the rate where NPV equals zero!
🧮 IRR Calculation Steps

Watch how IRR is calculated step by step:

1
Set up Cash Flows
Initial: -₹1,000
Year 1: +₹300
Year 2: +₹400
Year 3: +₹500
2
Set NPV equation to zero
-1000 + 300/(1+IRR)¹ + 400/(1+IRR)² + 500/(1+IRR)³ = 0
3
Trial and Error Method
Try 10%: NPV = +₹43.4
Try 15%: NPV = -₹28.9
4
Linear Interpolation
IRR = 10% + (43.4/(43.4+28.9)) × (15%-10%)
IRR = 13.0%
✓
Internal Rate of Return
13.0%
✔ Good Investment - IRR > Required Return!

If your required return is less than 13%, this investment creates value.
