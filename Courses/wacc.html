🧠 The Three Pillars of WACC
💳
Cost of Debt
Like credit card interest, but companies get tax discounts for interest payments
📈
Cost of Equity
What investors demand for taking company ownership risk - no guaranteed returns
⚖️
Capital Structure
The recipe: How much debt vs equity the company uses to fund operations
WACC = (E/V × Re) + (D/V × Rd × (1-T))
Where E=Equity, D=Debt, V=Total Value, Re=Cost of Equity, Rd=Cost of Debt, T=Tax Rate

🧮 WACC Assembly Blueprint

Watch how WACC is calculated step by step:

1
Capital Structure
Equity: ₹600 Cr
Debt: ₹400 Cr
Total: ₹1000 Cr
2
Calculate Weights
Equity Weight = 600/1000 = 60%, Debt Weight = 400/1000 = 40%
3
Component Costs
Cost of Equity: 12%
Cost of Debt: 8%
Tax Rate: 30%
4
Apply Tax Shield to Debt
After-tax Cost of Debt = 8% × (1 - 30%) = 5.6%
5
Calculate Weighted Components
Equity: 60% × 12% = 7.2%, Debt: 40% × 5.6% = 2.24%
